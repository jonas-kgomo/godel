How do we make text look so crisp on an 8K display while using almost zero memory? The answer lies in our **Multi-Channel Signed Distance Field (MSDF)** font engine.

## The Problem with Raster Fonts
Standard fonts are often drawn using "rasterization"—turning lines into a grid of dots. If you scale them up, they get blurry. If you bundle high-res images of every font size, your binary becomes huge.

## Our Solution: MSDF
**Gödel treats fonts like vectors.** We store a small "guide map" (an SDF) for each letter. When the GPU draws a letter, it looks at the map and says "I am 2 pixels away from the edge," and draws a perfectly sharp line.

### Multi-Channel (Technical Detail)
Standard SDFs round off sharp corners because they only store a single distance value. Gödel utilizes **MSDF**, which stores orthogonal signals across the R, G, and B channels.

<Info>
  **Expert Insight:** In our fragment shader, we sample these channels and calculate the **median**. This preserves the sharp intersection of edges, allowing for perfect glyph reconstruction even at low sampling frequencies.
</Info>

### Anti-Aliasing
We apply sub-pixel anti-aliasing via screen-space derivatives:
```glsl
float d = median(texture(sdf_atlas, uv).rgb);
float alpha = smoothstep(-fwidth(d), fwidth(d), d);
```
Result: Perfect text at any size, with a tiny app footprint.
