One of Gödel's biggest departures from standard rendering frameworks is how we handle shapes and text using **Signed Distance Fields (SDF)**.

## Infinite Zoom
Most apps use "bitmaps" (images made of tiny dots) for icons. If you zoom in too much, they get blurry or "pixelated."

**Gödel is different.** Instead of dots, we store mathematical descriptions of the shapes. This means our icons and text are "infinitely sharp"—no matter if you're on a 1080p monitor or an 8K display, the edges stay perfectly crisp.

### Why SDF?
1.  **Tiny Footprint**: Math take up much less space than high-res images.
2.  **GPU Native**: Shaders are extremely fast at calculating distance functions.
3.  **Perfect Consistency**: Renders identically across all operating systems.

### Mathematical Accuracy
We leverage screen-space width metrics to ensure that as you scale, the anti-aliasing remains consistent.
